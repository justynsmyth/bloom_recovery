## Overview

Bloom Recovery is a post-physical-therapy recovery platform designed to help patients continue progressing after discharge. The core idea is to bridge the gap between formal PT and full recovery with a structured at-home system that combines:

- a premium injury-specific physical recovery kit
- a digital progression and accountability layer
- continuity with the therapist or recovery plan after insurance-supported sessions end

The product is positioned as a continuation-of-care system rather than a generic wellness product.

## Problem

Patients who leave physical therapy are often not fully recovered, but they lose structured support once insurance coverage ends. At that point, they are expected to continue rehabilitation on their own with limited confidence, guidance, or accountability.

Common post-discharge issues:

- uncertainty about which exercises to do
- uncertainty about frequency and progression
- poor confidence in exercise form
- fear of reinjury
- recurring pain or incomplete recovery
- low motivation and low accountability
- lack of equipment at home
- inability to afford additional PT visits

Current alternatives are weak:

- printed home exercise sheets are easy to ignore, lose, or misuse
- extra PT visits are expensive
- YouTube, Google, TikTok, and generic fitness apps are not personalized
- personal trainers usually do not have PT-level expertise
- many patients simply stop doing the work and regress

## Opportunity

Bloom Recovery addresses a large and recurring problem:

- PT patients frequently need continued support after discharge
- reinjury and pain recurrence are common
- patients already demonstrate willingness to pay for effective recovery support
- telehealth and hybrid care behavior have increased since COVID
- outpatient PT capacity is constrained, while need is growing

The opportunity extends beyond recovery into prevention. Younger active adults and athletes may also use structured support to address smaller injuries before they become larger chronic problems.

- looking for reassurance, structure, and confidence

## Value Proposition

Bloom Recovery helps patients continue recovery with confidence after PT ends.

Primary value delivered:

- structured at-home guidance
- better accountability
- tailored recovery progression
- reduced fear of reinjury
- reduced travel and friction
- time savings
- stronger adherence to a plan
- a calmer, more premium recovery experience

In short, Bloom turns post-discharge recovery from an unstructured personal burden into a guided system.

## Business Model

Bloom Recovery evolves from an earlier concept into a more specific hybrid model.

### Current modeled business model

- one-time purchase of an injury-specific kit: `$349`
- recurring app subscription: `$20/month`

The physical kit includes mobility and recovery equipment plus curated care products. The subscription supports progression, reminders, accountability, and continuity of care.

### Earlier models explored

- D2C subscription around `$100/month`
- `$300` bundled program
- equipment upsells
- equipment rental around `$60`
- B2B2C clinic or therapist licensing / per-patient continuation models

### Strategic direction

The current direction is primarily B2B2C:

- partner with clinics
- get recommended at discharge
- reduce acquisition cost compared to pure DTC health brands

## Revenue Drivers

Key drivers from the financial model:

- number of clinic partnerships
- patient adoption per clinic
- conversion from kit buyers to paid app subscribers
- subscription retention duration

Modeled assumptions:

- 10 clinics in Year 1
- 250 total customers in Year 1
- about 25 patients per clinic annually
- about 5% adoption from eligible discharged orthopedic patients
- about 60% of kit buyers convert to the app subscription
- average retention of about 6 months

## Unit Economics

Modeled unit economics from the financial document:

- kit price: `$349`
- blended subscription revenue per customer: about `$72`
- total revenue per customer: about `$421`
- blended variable cost per customer: about `$124`
- contribution margin per customer: about `$297`
- CAC: about `$60`
- LTV:CAC: about `4.95x`

Important implication:

- the business does not depend on very long subscription lifetimes to recover acquisition cost
- the kit itself contributes enough margin to make the model structurally stronger

## Risks

### 1. Clinic adoption risk

Biggest early risk: clinics may adopt more slowly than expected.

Mitigations:

- revenue-share structure
- position Bloom as a patient-retention and outcomes tool
- validate with small pilot partnerships first

### 2. Regulatory and liability risk

Health-related guidance creates legal and operational constraints.

Mitigations:

- involve healthcare legal counsel early
- ensure licensed PT involvement where needed
- carry proper liability coverage
- use clear disclaimers and scope boundaries

### 3. Pricing / willingness-to-pay risk

Customers may hesitate if the product is framed like a generic wellness upsell.

Mitigations:

- position Bloom as structured continuation of care
- test pricing with landing pages and pilots
- highlight reinjury cost and the cost of additional PT visits

### 4. Working capital risk

Because this is a physical product business, inventory and fulfillment can constrain cash flow even with good margins.

Mitigations:

- monitor monthly cash flow, not just annual profit
- manage inventory carefully
- stage marketing spend
- preserve capital efficiency

## Funding Strategy

The financial plan estimates roughly `$200,000` in capital to reach a Year 2 milestone of approximately 1,250 patients.

Proposed allocation:

- `$75,000` for app development and refinement
- `$60,000` for marketing and clinic acquisition
- `$40,000` for inventory working capital
- `$25,000` operating buffer

Near-term strategy is lean bootstrapping before outside funding, with an emphasis on disciplined scaling and profitability rather than aggressive burn.

## Suggested Product Positioning

Based on the source material, Bloom Recovery should be described as:

> A structured continuation-of-care system for patients recovering after physical therapy discharge.

That framing is stronger than calling it a wellness brand or a generic recovery subscription because it ties directly to the real pain point, the clinical workflow, and the willingness to pay.

## Recommended Near-Term Priorities

1. Validate clinic demand with a small number of pilots.
2. Test whether the physical kit plus subscription bundle is more compelling than a digital-only offer.
3. Measure adoption, adherence, and retention before adding more product complexity.
4. Keep the first workflow operationally simple and clinically credible.
5. Treat inventory and cash flow management as core product constraints, not back-office details.
